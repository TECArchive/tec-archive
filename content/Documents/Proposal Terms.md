*Signaling Proposals*
Many communities have “suggestions” or “signaling proposals” which can be used to draw attention to a community issue but not themselves ask for any tokens.  In the interest of simplicity, and to avoid clutter, TEC requests that signaling proposals not be placed in the TEC dashboard, but rather to the TEC Discord Server for community discussion.

However, as described below, while signaling proposals are disfavored on theTEC dashboard, the TEC community will continue to use “abstain proposals” even though they do not ask for any TEC requests.

*Abstain proposal*

The abstain proposal can be used to raise the voting cap on all proposals by staking their TEC on the abstain proposal, therefore raising the level of support needed for any pending proposal to pass.

*Proposals*

Proposals are requests that ask the TEC Community to distribute the TEC token from the common pool.

These proposals will have the following attributes:

- A fee to create a proposal
- A period of time for which the community can vote on a proposal
- A delayed execution of any proposal to pass so that any alternative dispute resolutions, either Gravity or Celeste can be utilized
- ANYTHING ELSE?

When a proposal has received sufficient votes the requested amount of TEC will be transferred from the TEC common pool to the address identified by the proposal creator.

**Community Pledge:**

The Community Pledge describes the scope of how we agree to engage in the TEC community.  After Celeste is deployed, the Hatchers will vote on this Community Pledge.

TEC is a community of members seeking to build a better future.

TEC is a protocol where a digital currency, TEC, is issued and distributed programmatically. The TEC protocol does not narrowly define the set of activities that are valuable but instead relies on its community members to guide the distribution process by creating and staking on TEC distribution proposals.

The goal of the TEC protocol is to foster a healthy community by allocating a steady stream of TEC towards development, maintenance, and improvement of the common goods that bring the most value to the TEC community.

The growth of the TEC community is directly related to the community’s ability to foster an inclusive, welcoming, and healthy culture that people can feel proud of and delighted to adopt as their own. When people choose to participate in the TEC community, they are implicitly supporting the community’s culture, values, and norms.

Therefore, TEC rests on an ever-evolving social contract, which sets expectations for how members should engage both with the protocol and with one another.

*On-chain enforcement*

The on-chain mechanism for enforcement will occur through Celeste.

The ability for the TEC protocol to enforce this pledge is limited to the direct interactions that community members have with the protocol, specifically the ability for community members to submit proposals to allocate TEC or adjust protocol parameters.

A community member will be required to stake TEC (or wxDai) when submitting a proposal, attesting that the impact of the proposal could be reasonably considered to align with TEC social contract. A community member can create a dispute if they disagree with the proposer’s attestation by challenging the proposal and staking an amount of TEC (or wxDai). If after being challenged the proposal is not withdrawn, a decentralized oracle will be used to settle the dispute.

Participants in the alternative dispute resolution protocol are expected to review the proposal, this covenant, and related past disputes in order to provide a judgement that they feel best aligns with the established norms and intention of the TEC community.

If the subjective oracle responds in favor of the proposer, then the proposal will be unlocked and the challenger’s stake will be transferred to the proposer. On the other hand, if the dispute is resolved in favor of the challenger the proposal will be removed from consideration and the proposer’s stake will be given to the challenger.

*Off-chain enforcement*

While there is no canonical off-chain forum that represents the TEC community, there are community spaces including the TEC Discord, Forum and Github communities, and others forums which may emerge in the future which are expected to adhere to this pledge. TEC intends to use Gravity (see link [here](https://forum.tecommons.org/t/gravity-general-process/173)) and make use of its graduated sanctions in order to enforce community standards.

**Disputes:** Disputes in a DAO are to be expected.  The TEC utilizes two complimentary but distinct systems for alternative dispute resolution (hereinafter “ADR”).

Gravity is an internal ADR system wherein Gravitons are used to assist in deescalating internal conflict between community members. *Gravity*

The TEC provides its own mechanisms to address disputes. Proposals can be flagged by any member of the community through the [Gravity Typeform](https://the-commons-stack.typeform.com/to/rCVsK5RK) or directly on the comments section of the proposal in the forum. After 3 different flaggings, a member of the Gravity Working group or from the stewards Working group can approach the proposer to suggest adjustments or withdrawal of the proposal.

If the proposal continues and passes without the changes suggested, the proposal can be challenged by any member of the community for an arbitration process, using Celeste as the disputable voting mechanism.

*Celeste*

Celeste is an on-chain ADR governed by 1Hive community members.  Celeste is a Subjective Oracle, allowing smart contracts to ask questions and receive answers.

By invoking Celeste, a community member can align incentives between parties by ensuring that it is common knowledge that defectors from an agreed upon strategy will be punished.

Celeste is a fork of [Aragon Court](https://aragon.org/court), which is used for decentralized dispute resolution. The main difference between Celeste and Aragon Court, besides being on xDai and using Honey as its staking token, is that Celeste has chosen to integrate brightID in order to limit how much stake individuals can add to the system.

The Celeste protocol selects participants to evaluate a dispute and provide a resolution. They are selected proportionally to the amount of honey they have staked. Appeals can be taken.  As disputes are appealed the cost in terms of both time, capital, and attention increases, and as it does we can also expect the precedent set by the decision to have a greater impact and either build or erode significant trust in the mechanism as a whole. Essentially the process is designed to minimize the need to create disputes and to resolve disputes with minimal need for escalation, but as disputes do get escalated the incentive to provide the most appropriate response is amplified.
