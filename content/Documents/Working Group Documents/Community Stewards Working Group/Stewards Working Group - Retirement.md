**Stewards WG Retirement Document**

First we would like to express appreciation to those pushing in the direction of a small and singularly focused team for coordination. Notably and, among others.

Retiring the Stewards WG, and the role of Stewards in general, leaves a taste that is bitter sweet. On the one hand, the Stewards have provided much insight, perspective, diversity, leadership and coordination for the TEC for the past 2+ years. On the other hand, it became clear that a right-sizing was due, along with a need for more singular focus on our mission and sustainability.

Retirement Date
The Stewards WG was officially phased out as of October 31, 2022.  All documents and channels associated with the Stewards WG will be archived and remain on Discord, and our Gitbook.

WG Learnings
The Stewards were the active and present, servant leaders in the know within the TEC. We stated our goals as:

* **Shared vision**. To ensure all Stewards are aware of the other WG priorities and activities and are focused on our shared roadmap.
* **Effective coordination.** To plan and coordinate interdependent activities across multiple WG.
* **Maintain knowledge.** To maintain an active group of community stewards who are well versed in how the TEC functions and are ready to pitch in to help advance TEC goals.
* **Vision and Values.** Maintain alignment with the TEC mission vision and values.
* **Steward support.** To allow stewards to gather and discuss what we are feeling in the community and any issues or blockers they may be experiencing themselves or within their WG. This weekly space allows raised problems to be solved in a group setting with the benefit of collective intelligence.
* **Nurture Community.** Nurturing the overall culture/growth of the TEC community

And our WG was a place where Stewards could support each other, coordinate across our multiple workstreams and develop towards the mission of the TEC to advance Token Engineering.

Of the lessons, there is no better roundup that what [REDACTED] has pulled together from working session to collect [Steward and Working Group learnings](https://forum.tecommons.org/t/stewards-and-working-group-learnings/1175). The part about Stewardship, pasted below:

*Positives of Stewardship in the TEC:*

* *Stewards were enabled to develop leadership skills in their day-to-day engagements.*
* *The Steward structure enabled expertise and education to be present within community decision-making.*
* *The group became a reference for complex problems, and a trusted source for the advice process implemented within the TEC.*
* *Stewards lead Scrum events like the retrospective, which opened up an effective space for reflection and integration of what was and wasn’t working.*
* *Stewards provided mutual support for personal development to all members of the TEC.*
* *Stewardship coordination allowed for individual value alignment to occur as the community began to scale.*

  *Drawbacks of Stewardship in the TEC:*
* *We witnessed power being concentrated within the stewards group regarding some decisions.*
* *The TEC was Inward focused instead of outward facing to the large TE community.*
* *The work from Stewards became the most visible in the community, often overshadowing contributors.*
* *The Stewards created an echo chamber when it came to finding solutions.*
* *Stewards accumulated a lot of responsibilities that lead to work overload, and fatigue.*
* *There was a limited amount of TE expertise among the TEC Stewards.*

Financial
The Stewards WG multisig is still holding funds as of Dec. 8, 2022.  The current Stewards MultiSig Wallet holds 612.00 wxDai and has no past obligations.  The remaining funds will be sent to the Sampo WG Multisig and used in accordance with the responsibilities established by the Stewards WG.

Tasks & Functions

We consider the [Coordination Team proposal](https://forum.tecommons.org/t/tec-coordination-team-proposal/1177) a logical extension of the [Stewards WG Transformation proposal](https://forum.tecommons.org/t/the-steward-wg-transformation-proposal-for-community-advice-process/1118), but better refined by those stepping up to take active roles, and we have and will continue to support those efforts.

It remains to be seen whether the term “steward” will have meaning again in the TEC but for now our WG is officially retired and we welcome participation in supporting the coordination team.
