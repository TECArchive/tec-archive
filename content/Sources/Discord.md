# Discord

**Platform:** Discord — the TEC community server

Discord was TEC's real-time coordination hub. Day-to-day work, working-group meetings, quick decisions, onboarding, and announcements happened here. It complemented the [[Sources/Discourse]] forum: conversation and coordination on Discord, formal proposals and the durable record on the forum.

## Role in TEC

- **Working-group channels** — most working groups (Comms, Legal, Gravity, Transparency, Tech Spec, Hatch Outreach, Rewards, Stewards, and others) ran channels for ongoing coordination.
- **Onboarding** — new contributors were welcomed and oriented through dedicated channels and bots.
- **Announcements** — community-wide updates, call reminders, and governance notices.
- **GitHub activity** — a channel mirrored repository activity so contributors could follow technical work; see [[Code and Technical Artifacts]].
- **Praise** — much of the Praise contribution-recognition activity was dispatched and discussed in Discord; see [[Economics and Token Design]].

## What the archive captures

Governance-relevant and public channels are in scope for summarization and reference. The archive prioritizes channels that document decisions, coordination, and knowledge production.

## Scope and privacy

Out of scope: direct messages, private/role-gated channels, and personal data. Where Discord content is summarized, personal names are redacted per the [[About/Archival Policy]]. A server and channel allowlist will be recorded with the dataset exports.
